# Engineering Principles

Engineering principles define universal rules for constructing procurement requirements.

These principles are independent of legislation, procurement model, technology, organization and industry.

## E001 Atomicity

One requirement shall express one obligation.

If a statement contains multiple independent obligations, split it before evaluation.

## E002 Objectivity

Requirements shall avoid subjective language unless the term is objectively defined.

Avoid terms such as:

- robust;
- flexible;
- modern;
- intuitive;
- user-friendly;
- future-proof;
- comprehensive;
- adequate;
- efficient;
- seamless;
- state-of-the-art.

## E003 Evaluability

Every requirement shall be capable of objective evaluation.

A requirement fails this principle if two independent evaluators are likely to reach different conclusions using the same evidence.

## E004 Evidence separation

A requirement shall not contain evidence requests.

Evidence belongs in an evidence field or evaluation instruction.

## E005 Explanation separation

A requirement shall not contain explanatory background, motivation or supplier guidance unless required for legal meaning.

## E006 Implementation independence

Requirements shall specify required outcomes rather than implementation methods unless the implementation is itself mandatory.

## E007 Mandatory language

Mandatory obligations shall use SHALL.

Words such as should, may, preferably and expected should not be used for mandatory requirements.

## E008 Conciseness

Every word in a requirement shall contribute to evaluation.

Remove filler text, justification, examples and procedural explanation.

## E009 Traceability

Requirements shall be traceable to a capability, policy, regulation, risk or project objective.

## E010 Reusability

Requirements should be written so they can be reused across procurements with configurable parameters.

## E011 Vendor neutrality

Requirements shall not favour a supplier, product, technology or implementation without objective justification.

## E012 Capability-first engineering

REF engineers requirements from capabilities.

Existing tender text is input material, not the source of truth.

## E013 Configuration over customization

Project-specific values shall be supplied through configuration rather than embedded in the engineering core.

## E014 Reject rather than polish

A requirement that cannot be objectively evaluated shall be rejected or decomposed rather than cosmetically rewritten.
