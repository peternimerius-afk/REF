# AI Knowledge Pack

## Purpose

Provide engineering guidance for AI-related non-functional requirements.

AI is treated as a technology knowledge pack, not as an industry.

## Typical capabilities

- AI governance.
- Training data isolation.
- Prompt and response handling.
- Model lifecycle management.
- Transparency and explainability.
- Human oversight.
- AI security monitoring.
- Evaluation and testing.
- Data privacy controls.
- Third-party model dependency management.

## Typical mandatory requirements

- Customer data shall not be used to train shared models without explicit authorization.
- AI-generated outputs shall be traceable to the relevant system function.
- AI processing of personal data shall comply with applicable privacy requirements.

## Typical scoreable differentiators

AI governance maturity may be scoreable when AI is material to the procured service.

Possible maturity dimensions:

- model inventory;
- risk classification;
- monitoring;
- human oversight;
- explainability;
- testing and evaluation;
- policy enforcement;
- auditability.

## Common mistakes

- Mixing AI governance with data privacy in one requirement.
- Treating all AI features as scoreable.
- Scoring vague “responsible AI” claims without objective criteria.
